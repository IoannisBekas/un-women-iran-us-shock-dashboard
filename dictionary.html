How to use

Reference definitions for dashboard users

Use these definitions when interpreting the dashboard in analytical and briefing settings. The wording keeps a clear line between measured evidence, proxy indicators and model-based prioritization.

Food security

Food-security terms

Terms used to describe severity, consumption and coping outcomes.

Classification

IPC

Integrated Food Security Phase Classification. It is a standard framework for classifying acute food-insecurity severity.

Used here as total-population food-security severity, not as women/girls-specific data.

Severity threshold

IPC Phase 3+

Population in Crisis or worse food insecurity. Phase 3+ includes Phase 3 Crisis, Phase 4 Emergency and Phase 5 Catastrophe/Famine.

Used as a core prioritization signal, but not treated as sex-disaggregated.

IPC scale

IPC Phases 1-5

Phase 1 Minimal, Phase 2 Stressed, Phase 3 Crisis, Phase 4 Emergency and Phase 5 Catastrophe/Famine.

Higher phases indicate more severe acute food insecurity.

WFP outcome

FCS

Food Consumption Score. A WFP indicator summarizing household dietary diversity and food consumption frequency.

Public FCS data are used only where available and are not assumed to be sex-disaggregated.

Coping indicator

rCSI

Reduced Coping Strategy Index. A WFP indicator that reflects how often households use food consumption coping strategies.

Higher coping can indicate stress, but it needs context and survey coverage checks.

Coping indicator

LCS

Livelihood Coping Strategies. A measure of whether households use stress, crisis or emergency coping actions that may damage future resilience.

Useful when available, but not consistently public across all 28 countries.

Gender lens

Gender and labour terms

Terms used to keep UN Women interpretation clear and evidence-based.

Data standard

SADD

Sex- and age-disaggregated data. This means outcomes are broken down by sex and age group.

Needed before making measured claims about girls or women affected.

Model component

Gender vulnerability

Structural and proxy evidence that women and girls may have less capacity to absorb food, price, income and displacement shocks.

Uses gender/labour indicators; it is not a measured women/girls food-security outcome.

Priority score

Gender proxy priority

A prioritization score that combines the exposure score with gender vulnerability, IPC, WFP outcome availability, internal displacement, assistance pressure and selected price, import and agriculture pressures.

The added displacement and assistance weights reflect plausible differential gender effects; they do not estimate causal impact.

Labour indicator

Informal employment

Work that is not fully covered by formal labour protections, contracts, taxation or social insurance systems.

Used as a structural vulnerability input, especially where women rely on informal work.

Labour indicator

Vulnerable employment

Employment as own-account workers or contributing family workers. It often signals weaker income security and fewer protections.

Different from informal employment, but useful as a proxy when informality is missing.

Gendered exposure

Female agricultural employment

The share of employed women working in agriculture.

Used to understand exposure to agricultural input, fertilizer and food-production shocks.

Displacement

Displacement terms

Terms used to distinguish the continuity proxy from the current internal-displacement layer.

Continuity input

Legacy external displacement proxy

The older displacement pressure proxy retained so outputs remain comparable with the earlier dashboard version. It mainly reflects the displacement evidence available in that earlier workflow.

A zero here does not mean internal displacement is absent.

Model component

IDMC/IOM internal-displacement layer

The current displacement input built from public IDMC and IOM DTM evidence, including conflict displacement stocks, selected events and DTM summaries where available.

This is the field to inspect for internal displacement pressure in cases such as Palestine and Yemen.

Interpretation

Displacement pressure

A pressure pathway that can affect food security through movement, host-community strain, access constraints, loss of livelihoods and changed assistance needs.

Use both displacement fields together and read missingness flags before making claims.

Sources

Data-source acronyms

Public data platforms and institutional sources referenced in the dashboard analysis.

Platform

HDX

Humanitarian Data Exchange, a platform managed by OCHA for humanitarian datasets.

Used to access public food-price, IPC and humanitarian data files.

Agency

WFP

World Food Programme.

Source for food-price and selected public food-security outcome datasets.

Database

WDI

World Development Indicators from the World Bank.

Used for structural country indicators such as population, imports and labour variables.

Database

FAOSTAT

Food and Agriculture Organization statistical database.

Used for food security, agriculture and fertilizer-related indicators.

Agency

UNHCR

UN Refugee Agency.

Used for refugee and displacement-related population evidence.

Funding data

OCHA FTS

OCHA Financial Tracking Service.

Used for public humanitarian funding-flow and funding-pressure evidence.

Displacement

IOM DTM

International Organization for Migration Displacement Tracking Matrix.

Used for public mobility and internal-displacement summaries where available.

Displacement

IDMC

Internal Displacement Monitoring Centre.

Used for internal-displacement stocks and selected displacement-event evidence.

Labour data

ILOSTAT

International Labour Organization statistical database.

Used for informal employment and related labour-market vulnerability indicators.

Conflict data

ACLED

Armed Conflict Location & Event Data Project.

Important conflict-event source, but not included here because account/API access was unavailable.

Commodity prices

FAO Food Price Index

A global index tracking international prices of major food commodity groups.

Used as global food-price context, not as a country-level household outcome.

Commodity prices

World Bank Commodity Markets

World Bank source for international commodity price series.

Used for oil, fertilizer and food-price scenario context.

Model

Model and dashboard terms

Terms used to describe the index, scenarios and interpretation boundaries.

Index

Shock Exposure Index

A 0-100 composite screening score built from nine bounded component scores and documented weights.

Ranks exposure and monitoring priority; it does not estimate causal impact.

Index input

Component score

A normalized 0-1 measure for one pathway, such as food-price pressure, internal displacement or assistance pressure.

Components are weighted and summed to create the final index.

Calculation

Contribution points

The number of points a component adds to a composite score after its score and weight or coefficient are applied.

Used in the decomposition charts to show what drives the final score; it is arithmetic, not causal attribution.

Before/after

Peak increase

The highest positive median same-month food-price increase found in the available post-shock months.

This is the table's main price movement field; if no positive comparable rise exists, the row says so directly.

Before/after

Price records compared

The number of like-for-like WFP/HDX market, commodity and unit records behind the peak comparison.

A higher count means the price signal is based on more records; it is not a severity measure.

Context

Supplemental coverage

A count of five additional public-data context layers: ACLED, CPI, food incidents, PortWatch and Findex.

Shows what contextual data is available; it is not a proof score and does not replace the published index.

Method

Index weight

The percentage importance assigned to each component in the composite index.

Weights are documented, reviewed and sensitivity-tested.

Method

Scenario sensitivity

Stress tests showing how country risk scores move under oil, fertilizer, food-price or assistance-cut scenarios.

Useful for planning; not a forecast.

Method

Weight sensitivity

Alternative weighting assumptions that test whether country priorities change when the model emphasis changes.

Used as a robustness check for the documented-weight index.

Data quality

Data readiness

A check on whether the available data can support stronger analysis, such as before/after or causal estimation.

Most countries are not ready for women/girls causal estimates without SADD outcomes.

Interpretation

Causal estimate

An estimate that attributes a change in an outcome to a specific event or shock.

Not produced by this dashboard because required outcome data are unavailable.

Data handling

Proxy indicator

An indirect measure used when the ideal direct measure is missing.

Acceptable for prioritization when clearly labelled, but not a replacement for measured outcomes.

Data handling

Neutral imputation

Replacing missing component values with a neutral score, usually 0.5 on a 0-1 scale.

Prevents missing data alone from pushing a country to the top or bottom of the ranking.
