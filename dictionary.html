How to use

Reference definitions for dashboard users

Use these definitions when interpreting the dashboard in analytical and briefing settings. The wording keeps a clear line between measured evidence, raw/proxy indicators and the one user-facing composite score: the Shock Exposure Index.

Food security

Food-security terms

Terms directly used in the current dashboard to describe IPC severity, price evidence and WFP outcome availability.

Classification

IPC

Integrated Food Security Phase Classification. It is a standard framework for classifying acute food-insecurity severity.

Used here as total-population food-security severity, not as women/girls-specific data.

Severity threshold

IPC Phase 3+

Population in Crisis or worse food insecurity. Phase 3+ includes Phase 3 Crisis, Phase 4 Emergency and Phase 5 Catastrophe/Famine.

Used as a core prioritization signal, but not treated as sex-disaggregated.

IPC scale

IPC Phases 1-5

Phase 1 Minimal, Phase 2 Stressed, Phase 3 Crisis, Phase 4 Emergency and Phase 5 Catastrophe/Famine.

Higher phases indicate more severe acute food insecurity.

Model component

Public WFP food-security outcome layer

A bounded Shock Exposure Index component that uses public WFP food-security outcome evidence where available, such as poor/borderline food consumption or coping indicators.

The dashboard may show raw WFP fields where available; it does not treat them as SADD outcomes.

Gender lens

Gender and labour terms

Terms used to keep UN Women interpretation clear and evidence-based.

Data standard

SADD

Sex- and age-disaggregated data. This means outcomes are broken down by sex and age group.

Needed before making measured claims about girls or women affected.

Model component

Gender vulnerability

Structural and proxy evidence that women and girls may have less capacity to absorb food, price, income and displacement shocks.

Uses gender/labour indicators; it is not a measured women/girls food-security outcome.

Dashboard display

Gender/labour indicators

Raw or near-raw indicators shown directly for interpretation, including female informal employment, vulnerable employment, agricultural employment, labour-force participation and financial-access fields where available.

Overview charts show cross-country indicators; Country Lens shows selected-country values. They are not a separate dashboard score.

Labour indicator

Informal employment

Work that is not fully covered by formal labour protections, contracts, taxation or social insurance systems.

Used as a structural vulnerability input, especially where women rely on informal work.

Labour indicator

Vulnerable employment

Employment as own-account workers or contributing family workers. It often signals weaker income security and fewer protections.

Different from informal employment, but useful as a proxy when informality is missing.

Gendered exposure

Female agricultural employment

The share of employed women working in agriculture.

Used to understand exposure to agricultural input, fertilizer and food-production shocks.

Financial access

Women financial-access indicators

Findex indicators such as women's account ownership, mobile money account ownership, formal/mobile saving, wage receipt and government payment receipt.

Shown as raw or near-raw context for monitoring questions, not as a separate composite score.

Household structure

Female-headed households

The share of households headed by women where public country data are available.

Used as structural context for monitoring and data-gap assessment; coverage varies by country and year.

Displacement

Displacement terms

Terms used to distinguish visible public displacement evidence from internal index inputs.

Internal continuity input

Legacy external displacement proxy

The older displacement pressure proxy retained so outputs remain comparable with the earlier dashboard version. It mainly reflects the displacement evidence available in that earlier workflow.

A zero here does not mean internal displacement is absent; the dashboard now emphasizes raw IDMC/IOM evidence.

Internal model component

IDMC/IOM internal-displacement layer

The current displacement input built from public IDMC and IOM DTM evidence, including conflict displacement stocks, selected events and DTM summaries where available.

The user-facing dashboard shows the underlying IDMC/IOM values directly in an all-country displacement table.

Dashboard display

Public displacement evidence

Raw or near-raw public IDMC and IOM displacement values shown by country, such as conflict IDPs, selected DTM IDPs and selected post-shock displacement figures.

Large values indicate displacement relevance; missing values are data gaps, not evidence that displacement is absent.

Sources

Data-source acronyms

Public data platforms and institutional sources referenced in the dashboard analysis.

Platform

HDX

Humanitarian Data Exchange, a platform managed by OCHA for humanitarian datasets.

Used to access public food-price, IPC and humanitarian data files.

Agency

WFP

World Food Programme.

Source for food-price and selected public food-security outcome datasets.

Database

WDI

World Development Indicators from the World Bank.

Used for structural country indicators such as population, imports and labour variables.

Database

FAOSTAT

Food and Agriculture Organization statistical database.

Used for food security, agriculture and fertilizer-related indicators.

Agency

UNHCR

UN Refugee Agency.

Used for refugee and displacement-related population evidence.

Funding data

OCHA FTS

OCHA Financial Tracking Service.

Used for public humanitarian funding-flow and funding-pressure evidence.

Displacement

IOM DTM

International Organization for Migration Displacement Tracking Matrix.

Used for public mobility and internal-displacement summaries where available.

Displacement

IDMC

Internal Displacement Monitoring Centre.

Used for internal-displacement stocks and selected displacement-event evidence.

Labour data

ILOSTAT

International Labour Organization statistical database.

Used for informal employment and related labour-market vulnerability indicators.

Financial access

Findex

World Bank Global Findex database on account ownership, payments, saving, borrowing and financial access.

Used for raw women’s financial-access context where country coverage exists.

Conflict data

ACLED

Armed Conflict Location & Event Data Project.

Used only as additional public-context coverage where available; it is not part of the official Shock Exposure Index.

Commodity prices

FAO Food Price Index

A global index tracking international prices of major food commodity groups.

Used as global food-price context, not as a country-level household outcome.

Model

Model and dashboard terms

Terms used to describe the official index, supporting raw evidence and interpretation boundaries.

Index

Shock Exposure Index

A 0-100 composite screening score built from nine bounded component scores and documented weights.

Ranks exposure and monitoring priority; it does not estimate causal impact.

Official index input

Component score

A normalized 0-1 measure for one model component, such as food-price pressure, internal displacement or assistance pressure.

Components are weighted and summed to create the official Shock Exposure Index; other dashboard panels show raw values directly.

Calculation

Contribution points

The number of points a component adds to the Shock Exposure Index after its score and weight are applied.

Used only for the official index decomposition; it is arithmetic, not causal attribution.

Before/after

Peak increase

The highest positive median same-month food-price increase found in the available post-shock months.

This is the table's main price movement field; if no positive comparable rise exists, the row says so directly.

Before/after

Price records compared

The number of like-for-like WFP/HDX market, commodity and unit records behind the peak comparison.

A higher count means the price signal is based on more records; it is not a severity measure.

Context

Public-context coverage

Evidence-availability readouts showing whether selected public source families are available, partial or missing for a country.

Coverage explains source availability and data gaps; it is not a risk score and does not replace the published index.

Method

Index weight

The percentage importance assigned to each component in the composite index.

Weights are documented, reviewed and sensitivity-tested.

Data quality

Data confidence

A dashboard label describing how much caution is needed because of missing, neutral or incomplete source inputs.

Use it to qualify interpretation of the Shock Exposure Index and raw indicators; it is not a separate risk score.

Data quality

Readiness checks

A check on whether the available data can support stronger analysis, such as before/after or causal estimation.

Most countries are not ready for women/girls causal estimates without SADD outcomes.

Interpretation

Causal estimate

An estimate that attributes a change in an outcome to a specific event or shock.

Not produced by this dashboard because required outcome data are unavailable.

Data handling

Proxy indicator

An indirect measure used when the ideal direct measure is missing.

Acceptable for prioritization when clearly labelled, but not a replacement for measured outcomes.

Data handling

Neutral imputation

Replacing missing component values with a neutral score, usually 0.5 on a 0-1 scale.

Prevents missing data alone from pushing a country to the top or bottom of the ranking.

Internal/model traceability

Terms retained for workbook and methodology traceability

These terms may appear in hidden workbook sheets, generated data fields or methodology notes. They are not separate user-facing dashboard outputs or additional published scores.

Hidden/internal field

Internal gender-priority proxy

An earlier internal prioritization field retained in hidden data/workbook traces for reproducibility.

Do not cite it as a current dashboard output; the visible gender section now shows raw or near-raw indicators.

Hidden/internal component

Displacement pressure

An internal model component describing how displacement can affect food security through movement, host-community strain, access constraints, livelihood disruption and assistance needs.

The visible dashboard shows public IDMC/IOM evidence and the official Shock Exposure Index instead of a standalone displacement score.

Internal sensitivity check

Scenario sensitivity

Stress-test rows showing how model scores would move under oil, fertilizer, food-price or assistance-cut assumptions.

Retained for audit/sensitivity checks; not a forecast and not a user-facing dashboard score.

Internal sensitivity check

Weight sensitivity

Alternative weighting assumptions used to test whether country priorities change when model emphasis changes.

Internal robustness check for the documented-weight index, not a user-facing score.

WFP survey term

FCS

Food Consumption Score, a WFP indicator summarizing household dietary diversity and food consumption frequency.

Mentioned for outcome-data traceability where public WFP data exist; not treated as SADD in this dashboard.

WFP survey term

rCSI

Reduced Coping Strategy Index, a WFP indicator of how often households use food-consumption coping strategies.

Mentioned for outcome-data traceability; coverage varies and it is not a user-facing score.

WFP survey term

LCS

Livelihood Coping Strategies, a measure of stress, crisis or emergency coping actions.

Used as an example of SADD outcome data that should be requested before making gender-impact claims.

Internal scenario source

World Bank Commodity Markets

World Bank source for international commodity price series.

Referenced for internal oil, fertilizer and food-price scenario context; not a country-level household outcome.
