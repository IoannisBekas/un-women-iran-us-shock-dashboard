The index combines price pressure, import exposure, agriculture inputs, IPC severity, displacement, assistance pressure and gender vulnerability.

Higher bars mean higher relative exposure across the weighted model components. Bar outlines and the flags below mark countries where public inputs are missing or neutral-imputed. The 0-100 index supports prioritization; it is not a probability, forecast or measured causal impact.

Data-confidence flags identify countries where missing public inputs make interpretation more cautious; a flag is not evidence of lower risk.

    Descriptive price evidence

    Comparable food-price records show the largest positive rises in Sudan, Ethiopia, South Sudan and Somalia

    The food-price comparison input compares like-for-like WFP/HDX market and commodity price records in post-shock 2026 months with the same calendar month in 2025. This is a descriptive screening signal, not evidence that the Iran-USA escalation caused the observed price movement. Local conflict, inflation, exchange-rate movement, market access and seasonality can drive the same price series.

      Gender and labour context

      Raw gender and labour indicators by country

      These are country-level structural indicators for monitoring context. They are not separate dashboard scores, and they are not measured women/girls food-security outcomes.

      Female informal employment is highest where available

      Bars show the female informal employment percentage from ILOSTAT/proxy records where available. This is a raw labour-market indicator, not a separate dashboard score.

      Female vulnerable employment highlights income insecurity

      Bars show the share of employed women in vulnerable employment where available. This is a raw structural labour indicator, not a measured food-security outcome.

      Low female labour-force participation is highest where available

      Bars show the participation gap, calculated as 100 minus female labour-force participation. Longer bars mean lower participation and higher structural economic-access concern.

      Female agricultural employment shows exposure to food-system shocks

      Bars show the share of employed women working in agriculture where available. Use this as context for agriculture, fertilizer and food-production exposure.
