Food prices rose most sharply in Sudan, Ethiopia, South Sudan and Somalia

The food-price comparison input compares like-for-like WFP/HDX market and commodity price records in post-shock 2026 months with the same calendar month in 2025, then keeps the highest positive observed increase per country. Flat, negative and unavailable countries remain visible so readers can distinguish a price rise from a flat comparison or a missing price comparison. The country evidence keeps peak increase and IPC latest timing together where public records allow comparison.

    Sudan and South Sudan top the exposure-priority ranking

    The index combines price pressure, import exposure, agriculture inputs, IPC severity, displacement, assistance pressure and gender vulnerability.

    Higher bars mean higher relative exposure across the weighted model components. The 0-100 index supports prioritization; it is not a probability, forecast or measured causal impact.

    Data-confidence flags identify countries where missing public inputs make interpretation more cautious; a flag is not evidence of lower risk.

    Decision readout

    What this means

      East Africa has the highest average exposure score

      Bars show the average Shock Exposure Index score for countries in each region. Click a region bar to filter the dashboard to that region.

      Female informal employment is highest where available

      Bars show the female informal employment percentage from ILOSTAT/proxy records where available. This is a raw labour-market indicator, not a separate dashboard score.

      Female vulnerable employment highlights income insecurity

      Bars show the share of employed women in vulnerable employment where available. This is a raw structural labour indicator, not a measured food-security outcome.

      Female labour-force participation is lowest where available

      Bars show the female labour-force participation percentage, sorted from lowest to higher participation. Lower participation is used in the gender vulnerability component as a structural economic-access signal.

      Female agricultural employment shows exposure to food-system shocks

      Bars show the share of employed women working in agriculture where available. Use this as context for agriculture, fertilizer and food-production exposure.

      Evidence coverage

      Which public source families are available by country

      This readout shows source coverage, not risk. A source marked available means the current dashboard dataset contains usable evidence for that country; missing coverage is a data gap, not evidence that the issue is absent.

      ACLED, CPI, food incidents, PortWatch and Findex are public-context checks. They help explain data availability and context; they do not replace the Shock Exposure Index or create additional scores.
