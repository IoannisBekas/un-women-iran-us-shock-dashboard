    Score mechanics

    How the Shock Exposure Index is built

    The official composite score is still shown transparently. Other user-facing panels use raw or near-raw indicators instead of secondary proxy scores.

    Food security, displacement, assistance and structural indicators

    These are source indicators or near-raw dashboard fields. They are shown directly so readers do not have to interpret extra scores.

    How component scores become index points

    This is the only composite calculation shown to users: component score x component weight x 100. It explains the official index, not causal attribution.

    Selected-country gender and displacement lens

    Gender, IPC, assistance and internal displacement indicators

    This panel shows raw or near-raw indicators directly. IPC is total population; internal displacement comes from public IDMC/IOM evidence; gender indicators are structural context, not measured SADD outcomes.

    Female labour and financial-access indicators

    These indicators are visible context for UN Women monitoring. They are structural/proxy evidence, not measured SADD food-security outcomes.

    What public-context coverage adds

    Public-context coverage counts five additional public-source checks: ACLED availability, CPI, food incidents, PortWatch port activity and Findex gender-finance data. These layers explain data availability and context; they do not replace the published exposure score.
